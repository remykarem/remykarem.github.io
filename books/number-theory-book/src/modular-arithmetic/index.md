# Modular arithmetic

## Congruence

$a$ is said to be **congruent** to $b$ modulo $m$.

$$
a \equiv_m b
$$

or

$$
a \equiv b (\bmod m)
$$

or 

$$
a = b + km
$$

```admonish note title="Example"
For a congruence

$$
x \equiv_{12} 4
$$

Possible values of $x$:

$$
4 \equiv_{12} 4 \\
16 \equiv_{12} 4 \\
28 \equiv_{12} 4 \\
...
$$
```

## Modular multiplication

$$
38 \equiv_m 14 \\
38c \equiv_m 14c
$$
