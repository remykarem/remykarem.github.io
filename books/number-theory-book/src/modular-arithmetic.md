# Modular arithmetic

$$
38 \equiv 14 (\bmod 12)
$$

means $(38-14)$ is a multiple of 12.

So

$$
a \equiv b (\bmod n)
$$

```admonish note
ðŸ’¡ Here, $a$ and $b$ are said to be **congruent modulo** $n$.
```

can also be written as

$$
a = b + kn
$$

Exponentiation

$$
a^b (\bmod n) = (a (\bmod n))^b \mod n
$$

## Congruence

$$
x \equiv 2 (\bmod 3)
$$

Possible value of $x$:

$$
-4 = 2 +3\times-2 \\
-1 = 2 +3\times-1 \\
2 = 2 +3\times0 \\
5 = 2 +3\times1 \\ 
8 = 2 +3\times2 \\
11 = 2 +3\times2 \\
14 = 2 +3\times2 \\
17 = 2 +3\times2 \\
20 = 2 +3\times2 \\
...
$$
